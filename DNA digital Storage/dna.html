How to convert digital data and store in DNA?

There are more than 6 possible methods which can be used to convert the digital data into nucleotides, they are

  • Binary-Base encoding
  • Huffman Coding
  • Hamming codes and error correction
  • Fountain Codes
  • DNA alphabet expansion
1. Binary-Base encoding:

Binary-to-base encoding is a process used in bioinformatics to convert digital data, typically represented as binary sequences (0s and 1s), into sequences of nucleotides (A, T, G, and C) that can be used to represent genetic information, such as DNA or RNA

  • 00 maps to A
  • 01 maps to T
  • 10 maps to G
  • 11 maps to C

For example, let's say you want to encode the binary sequence "00101110" using the mapping provided earlier: So, the binary sequence "00101110" would be encoded as the nucleotide sequence "A(00)G(10)C(11)G(10)." We strat calculating this from right to left. Like how we do in binary to decimal conversion.

Why use DNA?

Have you ever pondered the choice of DNA amidst the plethora of available options in modern science and technology? It's a question that encourages contemplation and sparks curiosity about the rationale behind selecting DNA as a fundamental building block in various fields.

  • Volume - The amount of data which a DNA molecule can store is quite humungous, that is because we know that the molecules are very very small and very store a lot of data in a gram of DNA as compared to other storage devices, It is estimated that 1 gram of DNA can hold up to ~215 petabytes (1 petabyte = 1 million gigabytes) of information. Which cannot be achieved in any other modern storage devices.
  • Longevity - Once if we store the information inside the DNA then it can store data within it atleast for more than hundreds of years and even this cannot be achieved in modern day storage devices.
  • Environmental Impact - Unlike other devices we are not going to pollute the environment while the preparation of the DNA molecule or else while discarding the unused storage devices it pollutes the environment if not recycled.
  • Information Security - DNA storage offers unique advantages in terms of information security due to its density, biological complexity, and durability. However, it's not immune to security risks, and access control, physical security, and privacy concerns must be addressed to ensure the protection of the data stored within DNA molecules.

def binary_to_dna(binary_string):
  dna_sequence = ""
  binary_length = len(binary_string)

  # Ensure the binary string length is a multiple of 2
  if binary_length % 2 != 0:
      binary_string = "0" + binary_string  # Pad with a leading 0 if needed

  # Define a mapping from binary to DNA bases
  binary_to_dna_mapping = {
      "00": "A",
      "01": "C",
      "10": "G",
      "11": "T"
  }

  # Iterate through the binary string in chunks of 2
  for i in range(0, binary_length, 2):
      chunk = binary_string[i:i + 2]
      dna_base = binary_to_dna_mapping[chunk]
      dna_sequence += dna_base

  return dna_sequence

# Example usage
binary_data = "1101101001110010"  # Replace with your binary data
dna_sequence = binary_to_dna(binary_data)
print("Binary:   ", binary_data)
print("DNA Seq.: ", dna_sequence)
